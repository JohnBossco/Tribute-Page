MJ Legacy

  • Michael Jeffery Jordan was born February 17, 1963 to James R. Jordan and Deloris Jordan. In Fort Greene, Brooklyn New York City, But quickly moved with his family to Wilmington, North Carolina

  • Attended Emsley A. Laney High-School where he started his athletic career playing basketball, baseball, and football. He tried out for varsity team during his sophomore year at 5'11 but was cut. The following summer he grew 4 inches and practiced rigorously. He then earned a varsity spot and became a star player

  • Jordan Junior and Senior years were a break out season, and after colleges and university were ready to recruit him. A few were Duke, North Carolina, South Carolina, Syracuse and Virginia. He would go on to accept a scholarship at university of North Carolina

  • During His Freshman year Jordan was coached by Dean Smith, under his coaching he would go to be named ACC Freshman of the year. During his sophomore (1983) and Junior (1984) year was selected by consensus to the NCAA All-American First Team. Jordan would then leave North Carolina and enter the 1984 NBA Draft

  • Jordan was selected by the Chicago Bulls with the third overall pick only behind Hakeem Olajuwon, and Sam Bowie. Jordan would make his NBA debut at Chicago Stadium on October 26, 1984 scoring 16 points.During Jordans rookie he ended averaging 28.2 ppg on 51.5% shooting. He also quickly became a fan favorite being described as "The Phenomenal rookie of the Bulls"

  • Jordan would make the All-star game as rookie being voted in by the fans but this lead to controversy with veteran players upset at the attention the rookie was getting. After this Jordan would end hos season voted NBA Rookie of the year

  • 1985-86 season was cut short due to jordan breaking his foot in the third game of the year leading to him missing 64 games

  • 1986-87 season Jordan fully fully recovered and had one of the most dominate scoring season in NBA history scoring more than 3000 point in a season, only other player to do that was Wilt Chamberlain. In addition he became the first NBA player to record 200 steals and 100 blocked shots in a season. He would then go to win his first MVP award and also NBA Defensive player of the year

  • From 1987 to 1990 season Jordan lead the league in scoring going 35.0 in 87-88 32.5 in 88-89 and 33.6 in 89-90 however during these seasons the chicago bulls would never make it to the finals constantly being stopped by the Detroit Pistons

  • 1990-91 season Jordan won his second MVP award this season the Bulls set a franchise record with a 61 wins in the regular season. They would then Make it to Easter Conference Versus the Detroit Pistons. They would sweep the pistons in a four game sweep and advance to the NBA final. This was also a first time the Bull made it to a final in franchise history. To face the Los Angles Lakers.

  • 1991 NBA finals Jordan and the Bulls won the series four games to one, and set a 15-2 playoff record. Jordan would win his first NBA Finals MVP award

  • Jordan and Bulls dominance would continue in the 1991-92 setting a 67-15 record and bettering there franchise record. Jordan would win back to back MVP awards. Then would make the finals again versus the Portland Trail Blazers this time winning the NBA championship in six Games. Jordan won his second Finals MVP

  • 1992-93 Season Jordan and the bulls would now face the Phoenix Suns in the 1993 NBA finals Jordan had lost his MVP status to rival opponent Charles Berkley who was on the Suns. The Bulls however would win the NBA finals making it the third year in a row. Jordan would than go again to be named Finals MVP

  • October 6, 1994 Jordan announced his retirement saying that he lost his desire to play basketball. This decision was also shaped by the murder of his father during a carjacking. He would later Honor his father by signing a minor league baseball contract with the Chicago white sox his father always envisioned him as a Major League Baseball player

  • March 18, 1995 Jordan announced his return to the NBA with a two word press release "I'M BACK". Despite his eighteen month leave from the NBA Jordan still played very well however, him and the Bulls wouldn't make the finals.

  • Jordan was now motived by playoff defeat and trained relentlessly for the 1995-96 season. The Bulls with jordan would have the greatest regular season record in NBA history 72-10. The bulls would go to win the NBA finals with Jordan again wining Finals MVP

  • 1996-97 and 1997-98 season Jordan and the Bull would win the back to back NBA championship versus the Utah Jazz in both 1997 and 1998 finals. Jordan would win finals mvp and in both series setting a record for most Finals MVP at six.

  • January 13 1999 Jordan retired for the second time, but a year later would return to the NBA, but not as a player but part owner and president of the Washington Wizards, Yet Jordans love for basketball couldn't be stopped. Jordan announced on september 25, 2001 we would return to the NBA and play for the Washington Wizards.

  • Jordans Washington run would only last from 2001-2003 before he would play his last game on april 16, 2003 in Philadelphia. He would receive a standing ovation from his teammates, opponents, officials, and the crowd.

  • In 2009 Michael Jordan was inducted in the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of fame. He would be apart of the class of 2009 as a individual and also in 2010 as a member of the "Dream team"
